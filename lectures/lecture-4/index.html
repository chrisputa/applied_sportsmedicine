1 Exercise 1 — Limits of Performance

(Section 2 describes the impact of ageing on physical performance.)

Key messages:

  1. Muscular performance is limited with a minimum and a maximum
  2. Muscular performance depends on age
  3. Muscular performance is intensity dependent
  4. Muscular performance is time dependent
  5. Muscular performance requires ATP
  6. Sooner or later, oxygen will be required for ATP formation
  7. Rapid ATP formation initially occurs without the need for oxygen
  8. Slow ATP formation takes place using oxygen
  9. High demand of ATP flow rates causes high demands of hormonal regulation
  10. High demands of ATP flow rates cause a metabolic acidosis if exercise lasts more than a few seconds
  11. High demands of ATP flow rates require glucose as energy-delivering substrate
  12. Long-lasting ATP flow rates predominantly require fats as energy-delivering nutrients
  13. Under certain circumstances amino acids may contribute to ATP flow rates
