## One-Minute-Paper Topics
<Note>
  A **One-Minute-Paper** (OMP) is a short, focused prompt that students answer in ~60 seconds at the end of a session to consolidate learning, surface misconceptions, and provide formative feedback.  When answering, be concise, specific, and use terminology from today's session.
</Note>

### OMP 1 — The Exercise Infection Recovery Questionnaire (EIRQ)
The EIRQ is a structured clinical tool designed specifically to guide post-infection exercise decision-making in athletes and physically active individuals.

- What clinical gap does the EIRQ fill that was not addressed by earlier return-to-sport protocols?
- What are the main domains you would expect a post-infection exercise questionnaire to cover, based on what you know from the preceding lectures?

---

### OMP 2 — Purpose and Scope of Post-Infection Decision Tools
The EIRQ integrates evidence from current return-to-sport recommendations, graduated return-to-play protocols, and composite symptom severity scoring.

- Why is it insufficient to rely on a single criterion (e.g., "symptom-free for 3 days") for return-to-sport decisions after infection?
- What types of professionals should be involved in administering and interpreting a tool like the EIRQ?

---

### OMP 3 — Composite Symptom Severity Scoring (Diaz et al. 2025)
Composite symptom severity scores based on self-reports have been validated as predictors of SARS-CoV-2 infection and viral load.

- What is the value of a composite symptom severity score over individual symptom ratings (e.g., just checking for fever)?
- How could a composite symptom severity score be used in a practical screening protocol for athletes before returning to group training?

---

### OMP 4 — Graduated Return-to-Play (Elliott et al. 2020)
The Elliott et al. (2020) graduated return-to-play protocol provides a stepwise reintegration framework after COVID-19 infection.

- Describe the key principles of a graduated return-to-play protocol. Why are incremental steps important immunologically?
- What criteria should determine whether an athlete advances to the next step in the graduated protocol?

---

### OMP 5 — Evidence from Return-to-Sport After COVID-19 (Haunhorst et al. 2023)
A scoping review by Haunhorst et al. (2023) synthesised current return-to-sport recommendations after non-severe COVID-19 from an exercise immunology perspective.

- What does the term "scoping review" mean methodologically, and what can — and cannot — be concluded from this type of evidence?
- What are the main recommendations that emerged from the scoping review by Haunhorst et al. (2023) for post-COVID-19 return to sport?

---

### OMP 6 — The Case Study Method in Applied Sports Medicine
The EIRQ is applied using a structured case study approach in this lecture to translate immunological principles into practical clinical decisions.

- What are the advantages of the case study method for teaching clinical decision-making in sports medicine compared to purely theoretical instruction?
- What information would you need to collect from a patient history to effectively apply the EIRQ in a clinical consultation?

---

### OMP 7 — Post-Infection Exercise Decision-Making: Key Principles
The decision to return to exercise after infection must balance the immunological recovery trajectory against training load demands.

- What are the three most important immunological considerations that should delay return to exercise after an acute viral infection?
- How does the concept of "energy allocation" (the body prioritising immune defence over athletic performance) inform return-to-sport timing?

---

### OMP 8 — Symptom Profile Interpretation
The EIRQ requires systematic interpretation of symptom severity profiles to translate clinical findings into training recommendations.

- How would you interpret a case where an athlete reports feeling physically well but still shows elevated resting heart rate and poor sleep quality?
- What symptom combination would represent a "red flag" requiring immediate referral to a physician rather than application of a graduated return protocol?

---

### OMP 9 — Exercise Immunology Principles Underpinning the EIRQ
The EIRQ is built on exercise immunology principles including the biphasic immune response to exercise, post-exertional immune suppression, and the temporal dynamics of infection resolution.

- How does the open window hypothesis (post-exercise immune suppression) inform the minimum rest period before returning to moderate exercise?
- Why is the timing of return to high-intensity training different from return to low-intensity activity from an immunological standpoint?

---

### OMP 10 — COVID-19 as a Model Infection for Return-to-Sport Frameworks
The extensive evidence generated during the COVID-19 pandemic has allowed more precise return-to-sport frameworks to be developed than previously existed for most respiratory infections.

- What aspects of the COVID-19 return-to-sport evidence base are likely to be generalisable to other viral respiratory infections, and what may be COVID-19-specific?
- How should sports medicine practitioners adapt COVID-19-based frameworks for athletes recovering from influenza or RSV infection?

---

### OMP 11 — Individual Variability in Post-Infection Recovery
Recovery trajectories after infection vary substantially between athletes based on infection severity, fitness level, training history, and individual immune competence.

- What individual factors should be assessed to personalise return-to-sport recommendations using the EIRQ framework?
- How would your clinical recommendation differ between a well-trained endurance athlete and a recreational jogger recovering from the same mild URTI?

---

### OMP 12 — The Role of Psychological Readiness in Post-Infection Return
Fear of reinjury, reduced confidence, and illness-related anxiety are psychological barriers to successful return to sport after infection.

- How does post-infection psychological state differ from the psychological readiness challenges typically discussed after orthopaedic injury?
- Should psychological readiness be formally assessed as part of post-infection return-to-sport protocols? What instruments would be appropriate?

---

### OMP 13 — Monitoring During Graduated Return
Effective application of graduated return protocols requires ongoing monitoring of symptoms, vital signs, and subjective wellbeing during each reintegration phase.

- What minimal monitoring variables would you implement during a 5-step graduated return-to-sport protocol, and at what frequency?
- What would trigger a downgrade (reverting to a previous step) in a graduated return protocol?

---

### OMP 14 — Post-Exertional Symptom Exacerbation (PEM) as a Contraindication
Post-exertional malaise (PEM) — worsening of symptoms following physical or cognitive exertion — is a specific contraindication to graduated exercise progression.

- How is PEM distinguished from ordinary post-exercise fatigue in a clinical assessment?
- Why does PEM represent such a fundamentally different challenge compared to standard post-infection fatigue?

---

### OMP 15 — Return-to-Sport Communication: Shared Decision-Making
Effective return-to-sport decisions require shared decision-making between the athlete, coach, and medical team.

- What are the practical challenges of implementing shared decision-making for return-to-sport in a high-performance sports context?
- How would you communicate a "not yet ready to return" decision to an athlete who is subjectively feeling well but does not meet objective criteria?

---

### OMP 16 — The EIRQ in the Context of Other Clinical Tools
The EIRQ supplements existing tools such as the WURSS, neck check rule, and graduated return-to-play protocols rather than replacing them.

- How does the EIRQ relate to and complement the decision algorithms presented in Lecture 2?
- What is the added clinical value of having a purpose-built tool like the EIRQ compared to adapting general overtraining/recovery questionnaires?

---

### OMP 17 — Non-Severe vs. Severe COVID-19: Different Pathways
Return-to-sport protocols differ substantially between non-severe COVID-19 (the focus of this lecture) and severe COVID-19 with organ involvement.

- What additional medical investigations would be required before return to sport in an athlete who had moderate-to-severe COVID-19 with respiratory involvement?
- At what severity threshold should return-to-sport decisions move from sports medicine to specialist cardiopulmonary or internal medicine care?

---

### OMP 18 — Exercise as Immune Support vs. Exercise as Immune Burden
Moderate exercise supports immune recovery; excessive exercise during active infection impairs viral clearance and can lead to complications.

- Using the J-curve framework from Lecture 1, where on the exercise intensity spectrum do the EIRQ graduated steps sit during each phase of return?
- What is the immunological mechanism by which high-intensity exercise during acute viral infection could worsen clinical outcomes?

---

### OMP 19 — Documentation and Clinical Record-Keeping
Systematic documentation of EIRQ findings and return-to-sport decisions is essential for continuity of care and medicolegal responsibility.

- What elements should be documented in an athlete's medical record following a post-infection return-to-sport assessment using the EIRQ?
- How could digital tools (e.g., apps, wearable data integration) improve the systematic tracking of post-infection recovery in athletes?

---

### OMP 20 — Translating Evidence into Clinical Practice
Bridging the gap between published return-to-sport guidelines and everyday clinical practice remains one of the central challenges in applied sports medicine.

- What barriers prevent sports medicine practitioners from consistently applying evidence-based return-to-sport protocols in practice?
- How would you design a brief educational workshop for coaches and team physicians to implement the EIRQ in a regional sports club setting?
